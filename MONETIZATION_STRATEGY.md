## Revenue Model Breakdown

### Revenue Stream 1: AdMob Banner Ads (Free Users)

**How it works:**
- Banner ad displays at bottom of screen for free users
- You earn money every time someone views or clicks the ad
- Typical rates: $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 impressions (CPM)

**Optimization tips:**
1. Keep free tier useful enough to retain users
2. Show ad prominently but not annoyingly
3. Consider adding interstitial ads after successful conversions
4. Test different ad placements for higher CTR

### Revenue Stream 2: Premium Unlock ($2.99)

**What users get:**
- Unlimited batch conversions (vs 5 image limit for free)
- Ad-free experience
- Lifetime access (one-time payment)

**Why $2.99?**
- Low enough for impulse purchase
- High enough to generate meaningful revenue
- Standard pricing for utility apps
- You keep ~70% after Google's 30% cut = **$2.09 per sale**

### Phase 2: Growth (Week 3-8)

**Goal: 10,000-50,000 downloads**

1. **Google Ads** ($100-500 investment)

   - Get 50+ 5-star reviews (ask users)

3. **Cross-Promotion** (FREE)
   - If you build more apps, promote each other
   - "Users who liked this also downloaded..."

4. **Influencer Outreach** (FREE/$)

   - Offer free premium codes for review

### Phase 3: Scale (Month 3+)

**Goal: 100,000+ downloads**

1. **Paid Advertising at Scale**

   - Add "Share this app" button with incentive

   - Referral system

3. **Press Coverage**

## Alternative: Subscription Model

**Consider changing to subscription after initial launch:**

Instead of $2.99 one-time, offer:
- **Weekly**: $0.99/week
- **Monthly**: $2.99/month  
- **Yearly**: $19.99/year (best value)

**Why this could be better:**
- Recurring revenue (more predictable)
- Higher lifetime value per user
- 100 subscribers at $2.99/month = $299/month = $3,588/year
- Only need 280 subscribers to hit $10,000/year

**Why stick with one-time:**
- Easier to sell ("lifetime access")
- Less churn (no cancellations)
- Better for utility apps (vs content apps)
